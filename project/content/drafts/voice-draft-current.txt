TITLE: Faked Bone
TIMESTAMP: OMIT
BODY:
The xray they sent me, the one in the plain brown envelope with no return address, it's faked. I know my own bones I know what was there and what wasn't there this one they sent it's a perfect forgery no not perfect because I see the edge I see the way the light catches the layer they put on it it’s like a film almost you wouldn't notice unless you were really looking for it unless you knew what you were looking for which I do. They want me to believe there's a fracture there. A clean break right at the distal end. But it's not real. It's a fabrication.

It's another one of their messages, just like the weird fliers about services I never asked for that show up, or the subtle changes in the landscaping on the street corner that only *I* notice. This is a very specific message this time. It's about me, my body. They want me to question myself. They want me to think I'm damaged in a way I'm not. It's a signal, a very deliberate signal designed to throw me off, to make me doubt what I know about myself, what I know about my own insides. But I don't doubt. I know this part of me was solid.

I remember the last time I saw an xray for real reasons. It was for the pet. After the accident. They said it was just a fall from the couch, but it wasn't. Nothing is just a fall. And now this, this faked image of *my* insides, delivered to my mailbox, like it's normal correspondence. It’s too specific, it means something. It's a threat. Or a promise. They want me to believe their version of reality, their version of my own body, but I can see the seams. I can see the wire. It's all connected, it always is.
