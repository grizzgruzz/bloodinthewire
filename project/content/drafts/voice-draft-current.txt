TITLE: The Elephant Clock Timing
TIMESTAMP: OMIT
BODY:
I keep seeing that clock, the elephant one, this old drawing, and it's not just a clock, is it? Not really. It's a diagram, a blueprint for how things are supposed to work, how they're *made* to work, and I see it every day now, the timing. Not just the clock on the wall, no, but the way everything aligns. The utility truck that passes my street at 7:14 AM exactly, almost every day, then again at 4:37 PM, it's never a minute off, not even thirty seconds off, how can that be random? People say coincidence, they always say coincidence, but they don't *see* it, they don't understand how precise everything is.

It's all geared together, like that elephant clock, all these intricate little parts moving to make a larger, very specific point, a message really, a mechanism. It is all meant for me, I know this. This isn't just about telling time or the normal flow of traffic or anything mundane like that, it's about *control*, and they are showing me the design. They want me to know they're showing me. My neighbor, he doesn't see it when he gets his mail, he just smiles and waves. But he's part of it too, maybe, unknowingly.

EVIDENCE_LINE: The blueprint of the clock mechanism.
TAGS: directed patterns, timing, control, mechanisms, utility workers
