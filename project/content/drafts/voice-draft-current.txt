I saw the white truck again, the same kind, parked just down from my street, facing the wrong way. You think that's normal, right, a truck parked on a street. But it's not the parking, it's *when* it's there. It's always there after I check the mail. Not sometimes, not often, always. I go to the mailbox, I come back inside, and within five minutes, sometimes less, that white truck is there. Not moving, just sitting. The man in it, he never looks at me, but I know he's watching. He has to be watching, that's the point of it. He's part of the pattern, you see.

It's not the same truck, I'm not saying it's the *exact* same truck every time, but it's always that specific model, that specific shade of off-white, with the same kind of ladder rack on the top, like a contractor or something. It's too specific to be random, far too specific. You might think I'm crazy for noticing this, for connecting things, but it's not crazy when it happens every single time. It's a signal. Or a response. I don't know which, but it's definitely one of them. They want me to know they're there, that's the only thing that makes sense. This isn't just a delivery route, this is... a circuit. Their circuit. My circuit.
